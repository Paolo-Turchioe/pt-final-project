Labor in the US

This website explores the spatial distribution of unemployment and laborforce participation rates by state.

Unemployment Rate vs Laborforce Participation Rate

While the unemployment rate measures how many people are looking for work; the laborforce participation rate measures how many people are out of work and not looking. If someone is too discouraged to actively look for work, they would not be included in the unemployment rate but would show up in the laborforce participation rate. A low laborforce participation rate is not necessarily a bad sign. Students, retirees, stay at home parents, among others are not considered labor force participants.

The Relationship Between the Two

Although the unemployment rate is near historic lows, the labor force participation rate is stubbornly high. Generally states with low unemployment have higher laborforce participation, though there are some exceptions. Alaska has both relatively high unemployment and high labor force participation, and the Southeast has very low unemployment and low labor force participation.
